Is NK tumor infiltration clonally restricted?

Single-cell mtDNA lineage tracing of natural-killer cells across matched tumour, metastasis, normal tissue and blood — GSE302113 (Liu et al.)

Plan 3 · reanalysis with the mtclone pipeline · 8 donors · all analysis local

Headline. NK tumour infiltration is clonally restricted in high-grade serous ovarian cancer but polyclonal / passive in lung cancer. Restriction is tumour-type-dependent, not a general property of NK tumour-infiltrating lymphocytes. The signal rests on two HGSC ovarian donors (one clean, one with a cell-definition caveat); all five NSCLC donors show tumour NK clones shared with blood at chance levels.

1. The question

When natural-killer cells are found inside a solid tumour, are they the product of a clonal-restriction event — a few NK clones seed the tumour and expand locally — or a polyclonal infiltrate passively drawn from the many clones circulating in blood? These are mechanistically distinct and imply different biology (local antigen-independent expansion vs. continuous recruitment).

Somatic mitochondrial-DNA mutations are heritable, cell-intrinsic barcodes: cells sharing a rare mtDNA variant descend from a common ancestor. This lets us define NK clones and read two orthogonal signatures:

  1. Signature 1 — within-tumour clone-size skew. If a few clones expanded locally, tumour NK should be more oligoclonal (higher Gini) than the same patient's blood NK.
  2. Signature 2 — tumour↔blood clone overlap (the seeding test). If expansion is local, expanded tumour clones should be absent from blood (tumour-restricted). If infiltration is passive, tumour clones should mirror blood.

A genuinely restricted tumour should show both. The decisive test is Signature 2, tested against a permutation null.

2. The dataset

GSE302113 — Liu, Satpathy et al., “Clonal lineage tracing of innate immune cells in human cancer.” (bioRxiv 10.1101/2025.07.16.665245; Cancer Cell 2026 10.1016/j.ccell.2026.05.006). The method is mtscATAC-seq — joint single-cell chromatin accessibility + mtDNA genotyping in the same cell — so it delivers both a cell-type readout (ATAC gene activity) and the mtDNA clonal barcode. It is, to our knowledge, the only public deposit hitting all four requirements at once: solid tumour + deep mtDNA + matched blood + NK content.

We ingested 8 donors spanning two malignancies. The middle letter of each donor ID is the cancer type: SU-L-* = lung (NSCLC), SU-O-* = ovarian (HGSC = high-grade serous, LGSC = low-grade serous). Author-called per-cell heteroplasmy was used directly (reanalysis begins at NK subsetting, not variant calling).

3. Analysis overview

Eight ordered steps, all built on the frozen mtclone core:

  1. Ingest — author heteroplasmy TSVs → per-donor AnnData (8 donors, all sites joined).
  2. NK identification — ATAC gene-activity (accessible NCR1/GNLY/KLRD1, closed CD3D/CD8A), the Liu criterion, via mtclone.classify.label_nk; a strict NK-not-ILC set carried in parallel as a sensitivity check.
  3. Power gate — count NK carrying ≥1 informative mtDNA variant per donor×site (the go/no-go).
  4. Clone inference — mtclone.clones.call_clones per donor, jointly across that donor's sites (a clone spanning tumour and blood keeps one ID), donors held separate.
  5. Signature 1 — Gini + normalized Shannon (Pielou evenness) per site.
  6. Signature 2 — directional clone sharing + a permutation null that shuffles site labels within each donor (clone trees are donor-private).
  7. Robustness — re-run the seeding test across variant-frequency and clone-caller settings; expose which calls survive.
  8. Consolidate — ordered scripts + this report.

6. Signature 1 — within-tumour clonality

Only 2 of 8 donors have tumour NK more clonally skewed than their own blood NK: SU-O-005 (Gini 0.77 vs 0.61) and SU-L-004 (0.82 vs 0.75). The other six — including the strongest seeding case, SU-O-004 — have tumour NK equal to or less skewed than blood. Robust to the NK threshold (strict NK-not-ILC gives the identical 2/8).

Reading: within-tumour oligoclonality alone is neither necessary nor sufficient for restriction. This already matches Liu's “mixed, tumour-dependent” description rather than a clean universal signal.

7. Signature 2 — tumour↔blood overlap (the seeding test)

The permutation null shuffles site labels within each donor (1000×) and recomputes the tumour↔blood clone-mixing statistic. Observed mixing below the null ⇒ clones are more site-segregated than chance ⇒ local restriction / seeding. Observed ≈ null ⇒ tumour mirrors blood ⇒ passive infiltration.

Result: all three ovarian donors are significant at baseline (SU-O-004 p=0.001, SU-O-005 p=0.001, SU-O-002 p=0.011); no NSCLC donor is (p=0.18–0.54). The signal tracks tumour type, extending Liu's single ovarian case to all three ovarian donors while showing lung NK infiltration is polyclonal.

8. Robustness & the fragmentation artifact

Not every parameter change is a fair test. We quantified how much each configuration distorts clone structure (median |log₂(clones / baseline-clones)|). Four configs preserve structure (baseline, max_pb=0.5, binarize=0.05, binarize=0.1); four distort it badly — dropping high-frequency variants (max_pb≤0.1) fragments clones (removing ~10 variants explodes clone counts, e.g. SU-L-005 11→205), and aggressive edge cutoffs either fragment (edge=0.7) or merge (edge=0.3) them.

The trap: at the clone-distorting settings, every donor including all NSCLC turns falsely “significant” — a sparsity artifact, not contamination control. The honest verdict therefore uses only the four structure-preserving configs.

  • SU-O-005 (HGSC, the Liu anchor) — robust to parameters (3/4) but loses significance under the strict NK-not-ILC filter (p=0.47). Real under the primary analysis, sensitive to how NK are defined — one honest asterisk.

The two signatures are partly decoupled. SU-O-005 has the strongest within-tumour skew but a softer cross-site signal; SU-O-004 has modest skew but the cleanest seeding. Restriction is best read from the segregation test, not from within-tumour Gini alone.

10. How this revises Liu et al.

  • Confirms & extends their central ovarian observation: HGSC tumour NK carry oligoclonal, tumour-restricted expansions absent from blood — and it is not a one-tumour fluke (SU-O-004 is a second, cleaner case than their SU-O-005 anchor).
  • Explains their aggregate finding that NK grouped with the innate, high-clone-sharing pattern: that aggregate is dominated by the lung tumours, where NK infiltration genuinely is polyclonal. The mixed picture resolves into a tumour-type split.
  • Adds a caveat they could not test at single-case resolution: the SU-O-005 anchor's seeding signal is sensitive to NK-vs-ILC definition, so the strongest evidence for restriction is SU-O-004, not the originally-highlighted case.

11. Key decisions & caveats

Decisions made

  • Both malignancies ingested (ovarian + NSCLC) and the omentum metastasis included as SU-O-005's third clonal site (user decisions).
  • Author heteroplasmy used directly — no re-calling from raw; reanalysis starts at NK subsetting.
  • Two NK definitions carried throughout (base + strict NK-not-ILC) rather than committing to one — this is what surfaced the SU-O-005 asterisk.
  • Normal/adjacent-tissue sites retained for joint clone-calling (a clone's identity is defined across all of a donor's sites).

Caveats (honest limits)

Power rides on the two HGSC cases (SU-O-004: 1,617 tumour NK with variants; SU-O-005: 2,013); NSCLC per-donor counts are thinner (125–470). Every conclusion is per-patient, not cohort-pooled — “ovarian seeding” means a consistent direction across ovarian patients, not a pooled significance test.

The ambient/doublet floor is only partial. The GEO processed layer has no per-cell depth, so a coverage-based contamination model cannot be fit; the variant-frequency sweep is the available proxy (and the robust ovarian calls do not depend on near-public variants). A full floor needs the raw mgatk output.

mtDNA clonality cannot separate “expanded in situ” from “arrived and proliferated” — both yield a large tumour-restricted clone. Layering the ATAC residency/proliferation programs is a follow-up.

External benchmark pending. The Rückert blood adaptive-NK oligoclonality yardstick (separate workstream) is not yet available; the within-patient blood-NK comparison served as the primary yardstick.
