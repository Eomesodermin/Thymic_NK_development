# Literature review — developmental origins of NK cells, TCR footprints, and tumor infiltration

_Grounded synthesis for the hackathon project. Every claim is anchored to a retrieved, DOI-verified source. Organized by the project's four questions plus the methods that would test them._

## The dual-origin model

The premise that CD56^bright and CD56^dim NK cells might be developmentally divergent rather than a single maturation continuum has moved from speculation to a position with direct experimental support. The most decisive recent evidence is from mouse: [Ding et al. 2024, *Nat Immunol*](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41590-024-01865-2) identified an NK-lineage-biased progenitor ("early NK progenitor", ENKP) that develops into NK cells **independently of the common innate-lymphoid-cell precursor (ILCP)**, and showed that ENKP-derived and ILCP-derived NK cells are transcriptionally distinct with different functional biases (ENKP_NK dominate the MCMV/Ly49H response; ILCP_NK are better IFNγ producers). Critically for the human argument, they reported that **human CD56^dim cells resemble ENKP_NK and human CD56^bright cells resemble ILCP_NK** — i.e. the two human subsets map onto two developmentally distinct murine pathways. This is the strongest published statement that bright and dim are not simply sequential stages. The underlying single-cell data (sorted lymphoid progenitors + mature NK/ILC1 from TCF-YFP-reporter mouse bone marrow, 10x scRNA with a CITE component) is public at [GEO GSE266109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE266109) — the natural cross-species anchor for testing whether a human TCR-footprint⁺ NK subset aligns with the ILCP (CD56^bright-like) side, as the model predicts.

This reframes but does not overturn the classical continuum model, which remains well-evidenced for *peripheral* differentiation. [Freud et al. 2006, *J Exp Med*](https://doi.org/10.1084/jem.20052507) defined discrete CD34→CD117→CD94 NK developmental intermediates in human lymph nodes and tonsils, establishing secondary lymphoid tissue as a site of terminal maturation. [Yu, Freud & Caligiuri 2019, *Front Immunol* ("One Road or Many?")](https://doi.org/10.3389/fimmu.2019.02078) explicitly frames the open question: the prevailing linear CLP→CD56^bright→CD56^dim model versus alternative branched routes. And [Yu et al. 2009, *Blood*](https://doi.org/10.1182/blood-2009-04-215491) used CD94 density to define a functional CD94^high CD56^dim intermediary "between" bright and dim, which is the kind of continuum evidence the project argues has been over-generalized from peripheral plasticity to developmental origin. The project's core epistemic move — that the field conflates *peripheral differentiation* (bright can adopt a dim phenotype) with *developmental origin* (bright and dim may descend from different precursors) — is precisely the tension left unresolved between these two bodies of work.

## Thymic and aborted-T-cell NK development

The thymic NK pathway is old and solid in mouse. [Vosshenrich et al. 2006, *Nat Immunol*](https://doi.org/10.1038/ni1395) defined a GATA-3– and CD127(IL-7Rα)-dependent thymic NK pathway producing CD11b^lo CD16^− Ly49^lo cells with reduced cytotoxicity and high cytokine output — explicitly noted as "reminiscent of human CD56^bright" cells. The companion review [Di Santo & Vosshenrich 2006, *Immunol Rev*](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1600-065X.2006.00461.x) laid out the bone-marrow-versus-thymus dichotomy and argued thymic NK cells are actively exported to the periphery — this is the Di Santo-group thread the project builds on. [Huntington, Vosshenrich & Di Santo 2007, *Nat Rev Immunol*](https://doi.org/10.1038/nri2154) reviews how these pathways generate NK diversity. The "intrathymic crossroads" of T-versus-NK fate from bipotent precursors is reviewed in [Klein Wolterink et al. 2010, *Immunol Rev*](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1600-065X.2010.00960.x).

The human bridge arrived recently and is directly on-thesis. [Reiß et al. 2025, *Sci Adv* — "A human NK cell progenitor that originates in the thymus"](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adv9650) identifies a thymic ILC1-like progenitor (thyILC1) that is **closely related by scRNA-seq to CD34^+ double-negative thymocytes**, is thymus-dependent (lost in patients with congenital thymic hypoplasia), and preferentially generates KIR^+ NKG2A^+ NK cells. This is arguably the single most important paper for the project: it establishes a human thymic NK developmental route branching near the DN thymocyte stage — exactly the "branch from early DN T-cell development" the project hypothesizes. The translational framing is reviewed in [Forbes et al. 2024, *ImmunoTargets Ther* — "Thymic NK-Cells and Their Potential in Cancer Immunotherapy"](https://doi.org/10.2147/ITT.S441639), which also notes that thymic NK cells express intracellular CD3 subunits **without a rearranged TCR** — a partial T-lineage molecular footprint short of productive rearrangement.

The important **counterpoint** the project must engage: [Ribeiro et al. 2010, *J Immunol* — "Thymic NK Cells Develop Independently from T Cell Precursors"](https://doi.org/10.4049/jimmunol.1002273) argues thymic NK cells arise from a dedicated NK-committed route, *not* from T-cell precursors that abort. So "thymic-derived" and "aborted-T-cell-derived" are not synonyms in the literature — the project's aborted-T hypothesis is a stronger and more contested claim than mere thymic origin, and the analysis should keep the two separate.

Distinguishing thymic from aborted-T origin — and anchoring any DN-branch trajectory — requires human thymic developmental references, and several public paired-VDJ or DN-stage datasets exist beyond the EGA-gated Reiß deposit. [Sanchez Sanchez et al. 2022, *Nat Commun*](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-33488-2) ([GSE180059](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE180059)) provides 5′ scRNA **with paired TCR** on sorted human fetal and postnatal γδ thymocytes — an in-thymus substrate for the TRG/TRD rearrangement-order logic that separates developmental stages. [GSE227408](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE227408) profiles paired blood + thymus single-cell AIRR-seq (TCR+BCR) in pediatric donors, allowing the thymic footprint to be compared against the periphery in the same individuals. For transcriptional DN-stage anchoring, [GSE195812](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE195812) (sorted DN1/DN2/DN3 human thymocytes) and [GSE206710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE206710) (pediatric CD3⁺/CD3⁻ thymocytes) provide the independent developmental axis an AIRR-feature pseudotime must be validated against — a caveat that matters because pseudotime on a high-dimensional feature space can manufacture a trajectory from noise unless anchored to an orthogonal marker axis.

## Partial / non-productive TCR rearrangement in NK cells

That NK cells can carry TCR gene rearrangements is a decades-old, repeatedly rediscovered observation. [Leiden et al. 1988, *Immunogenetics*](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00376117) showed human NK clones with rearranged TCRβ/γ, while demonstrating NK activity does **not require** TCR rearrangement — i.e. rearrangement is present in some NK cells but is not functionally needed, consistent with a developmental relic rather than a working receptor. Related NKR-repertoire work ([Uhrberg et al. 2001, *J Immunol*](https://doi.org/10.4049/jimmunol.166.6.3923)) shows NK and γδ T cells share highly similar NK-receptor repertoires and that TCR-rearrangement status and NK-receptor expression are decoupled. The project's contention — that these partial/non-productive rearrangements are systematically discarded by standard scTCR QC (which keeps only productive contigs) and dismissed as ambient contamination — is a methodological gap, not a claim contradicted by data.

The tool that makes this newly tractable is [Suo, Tuong et al. 2023, *Nat Biotechnol* — Dandelion](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-023-01734-7). Dandelion's stated advance is exactly the retention and annotation of **non-productive and partially spliced V(D)J contigs** that other pipelines drop, plus a VDJ-feature-space pseudotime that improved reconstruction of human thymic T-cell trajectories and, per the abstract, "provided insights into the origins of ... ILC/NK cell development." This is the "buried in the figures" NK/aborted-T signal the project references, and it is the natural engine for Q1–Q3. There is now also an R port, [dandelionR (2025 preprint)](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.04.636146).

Two points about the Dandelion paper matter directly for the project's methodology. First, **the ILC/NK-origin signal was generated on a specific, public, paired-VDJ atlas** — the [Suo et al. 2022, *Science* — "Mapping the developing human immune system across organs"](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abo0510) dataset (fetal liver, thymus, bone marrow, spleen, gut and other organs; paired scRNA + scVDJ; browsable on CELLxGENE and with the GEX component at ArrayExpress E-MTAB-11343). Reproducing and extending "the buried signal" means analysing this atlas, which spans the fetal thymus and so also provides an in-thymus substrate for Q1 and the DN-branch trajectory for Q3 — it was, however, absent from the original dataset pool. Second, the Dandelion authors deposited a dedicated control experiment ([ArrayExpress E-MTAB-12524](https://www.ebi.ac.uk/biostudies/arrayexpress/studies/E-MTAB-12524)) in which PBMCs were treated with cycloheximide to block nonsense-mediated decay: this stabilises the non-productive, NMD-targeted TCR/BCR transcripts that would otherwise be degraded, providing evidence that **non-productive contigs are genuine rearrangement-derived transcripts rather than sequencing artifacts** — the exact confound the hackathon's contamination controls must address.

## mtDNA lineage tracing for clonal restriction (Q4)

The clonality question is answerable with mtSNP methods that do not require genetic engineering. [Ludwig et al. 2019, *Cell*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2019.01.022) established that somatic mtDNA mutations act as natural clonal barcodes readable from scRNA-seq/scATAC-seq with simultaneous cell-state readout. [Lareau et al. 2023, *Nat Protoc* — mtscATAC-seq](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41596-022-00795-3) and the earlier [Lareau et al. 2019 approach (eLife/related)](https://doi.org/10.7554/elife.45105) give high-confidence mtDNA-variant detection; the analytical caveat that clonal expansion governs power is treated in recent genome-scale work. State-of-the-art application: [Weng et al. 2024, *Nature* — "Deciphering cell states and genealogies of human haematopoiesis"](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07066-z) uses deep mtDNA-mutation detection to build clonal phylogenies of human HSC output — the template for asking whether tumor NK cells are the progeny of few progenitors (clonally restricted) versus a diverse infiltrate. Non-human-primate barcode lineage tracing ([Wu et al. 2014, *Cell Stem Cell*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.stem.2014.01.020)) provides the orthogonal, engineered-barcode evidence for distinct NK-lineage clonal dynamics in primates.

## Datasets and annotation standards

For Q2/Q4 the annotation-confidence problem is decisive, and CITE-seq datasets are the defense. [Salem et al. 2026, *Eur J Immunol* — "Gene Regulatory Programs of NK Cells"](https://doi.org/10.1002/eji.70142) provides matched CITE-seq + snATAC-seq of human CD56^bright vs CD56^dim peripheral NK subsets, showing bright and dim have distinct distal-regulatory-element landscapes — a protein-anchored, epigenetically-resolved subset dataset directly usable to defend NK annotation and test H2/H3. Broad multi-tissue VDJ+GEX reference: [Domínguez Conde et al. 2022, *Science* — cross-tissue immune atlas / CellTypist](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abl5197) (~360k cells, 16 tissues, paired VDJ) is an obvious mining target for TCR-in-NK across tissues with a curated annotation model — and, being **healthy adult**, is a cleaner primary baseline for a per-cell excess-over-ambient rate than an activated/perturbed disease cohort. The single most on-thesis substrate, however, is the Suo 2022 developing-immune atlas above: it is the dataset the Dandelion NK/ILC-origin claim was built on, spans the fetal thymus, and carries the paired VDJ needed for both Q1 counting and Q3 trajectory. See `dataset_search_2_addendum.md` for the full round-2 dataset evaluation and revised selection. The canonical framing of human NK subset diversity that any annotation must respect is [Freud, Mundy-Bosse, Yu & Caligiuri 2017, *Immunity* — "The Broad Spectrum of Human NK Cell Diversity"](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.immuni.2017.10.008).

## Boundaries of the evidence

The dual-origin claim (H1–H3 in the abstract sense) is now **supported**, not speculative: two developmentally distinct NK pathways exist in mouse and map onto human bright/dim ([Ding 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41590-024-01865-2)); a human thymic NK progenitor branching near DN thymocytes exists ([Reiß 2025, *Sci Adv*](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adv9650)); TCR rearrangements occur in NK cells and standard QC discards exactly the non-productive contigs that would carry the signal ([Leiden 1988](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00376117); [Dandelion 2023](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-023-01734-7)). What is **not** established, and what the project cannot settle by dataset mining alone: (1) that partial TCR presence *proves* thymic/aborted-T origin (the 2010 counterpoint separates thymic from aborted-T origin); (2) that CD56^bright specifically is the TCR+/thymic-origin subset (correlational at best from scRNA); and (3) the tumor-seeding directionality (H4) — mtSNP can show clonal restriction but not origin. These are the honest boundaries the hackathon scope must respect.
